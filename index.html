Origami Instructions and Designs


Step by step diagrams are probably the most popular and easy to follow way to show how to fold things out of paper. Trying to fine good origami designs on the internet can be a lot of work though. To help your search we've put together the largest database of free origami designs anywhere on the internet.

Camel
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and other humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Chameleon
  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of iguana suborder.
  2. Changing skin colours is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Cicada
  • Cicadas Live on All Continents Except Antarctica.
  • They Only Emerge When the Ground Is 64 Degrees.
  • They Have One of the Longest Insect Life Spans.

Panda
  1. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  2. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.
  3. A panda’s paw has six digits—five fingers and an opposable pseudo-thumb
    (actually an enlarged wrist bone) it uses merely to hold bamboo while eating.

Pigeon
  • They understand space and time.
  • They saved many human lives during WORLD WAR I and II.
  • Pigeons produce fake "milk".

Teddy Bear
  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused
    to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. They aren’t just for children – 40% of teddy sales are for adults,
    and a quarter of us still has our childhood companion.
  3. The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
